# Principles

## Discovery Over Search

Help users find possibilities they would not have found alone.

## Confidence Over Quantity

A short, strong shortlist is better than a long, forgettable list.

## Every Recommendation Earns Its Place

Each name must have a clear reason to appear.

## Explain the Decision

A recommendation without explanation creates doubt.

## Taste Must Be Inferred

Users may not know how to describe their taste. The product must learn it from choices, reactions, and tradeoffs.

## Trust Is the Product

Meaning, pronunciation, popularity, cultural context, risks, and strengths must be handled honestly.

## AI Serves Human Judgment

The user owns the final decision. AI supports thinking; it does not replace it.

## Simplicity Wins

Reduce friction, cognitive load, and unnecessary features.

## Build Once, Reuse Deliberately

Shared capabilities belong in the platform. Vertical-specific value belongs in configuration and focused modules.

## Ship, Observe, Improve

Prefer small releases, real usage, and evidence over speculative complexity.
